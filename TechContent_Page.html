Career Background

My career progression hasn’t been a clear path into the Tech industry. I first started my journey in 2009 at 20 years old as a Management Team Member of a Not-for-profit organisation. Before that I worked in hospitality. The NFP company I worked for was heavily involved in developing sports programs and delivering coaching support to all members of the community. Other community engagements that were inspired by my Indigenous cultural background were also held such as ‘Woolkoom Sea Day’. This was a day when our cultural elder (my Uncle Merv) and the dance troop performed and a kappa-murri (underground oven) was prepared. I worked for the community association for 12 years. By 2021 I was in my last year of my Master of Business Administration (MBA) program. I found an opportunity online that was for Indigenous people who would like to learn data analytics. The ‘Data Analytics and Cloud Training’ bootcamp was a 6-month program held online by Goanna Education. I enjoyed learning skills in areas such as Azure, SQL, Excel, Python and Power Bi. My main goal after much consideration was to transition into a tech career. I now had a new passion and drive and with the assistance of Goanna Education after I completed the program I was offered a position at Accenture. Accenture is a global consultancy company. I spent a year with Accenture while learning the ins and outs of my Application Development Analyst role. While working on the project that I was assigned to I found an area of IT that I hadn’t encountered before which was ‘Cybersecurity’. I became interested in the processes and discussions that would come up with the cybersecurity personnel and found the concepts compelling. I watched many videos and read articles about different cybersecurity matters. I then decided to enrol in the Cybersecurity Bootcamp with the University of Sydney. I learnt many aspects of cybersecurity in the program and had exposure the following tools: Burp Suite, Nmap, Zenmap, Metasploit, Snort, Shellshock, John The Ripper, Wireshark, Sguil, Shodan, Hashcat, Nessus, Linux (Terminal, Bash Scripting), Windows (Powershell), Aircrack-NG, Steganography, OSI Model, Azure (VM's, Load Balancer, Resource Groups, Security Rules), Containers (dockers), Ansible Playbooks, OWASP, OSINT Framework (Domain Dossier). I thoroughly enjoyed the content in the program and had the opportunity to connect with other people who were interested in the area. The Cybersecurity Bootcamp allowed me to have a foot in the door with an opportunity with a major Australian Bank. I commenced work with the bank from October 2022. My role as an Indigenous Academy Technology Cadet has afforded me the option to work in the cyber squad. I have managed to work with two different teams in my rotation within cyber. I have been trained in cloud technology, risk and controls. I decided to apply for a position as a participant in the She Codes Australia Plus program to learn to code this year. The relationship between knowing how to code and being a skilled cybersecurity professional very strong. Top cybersecurity professionals know how to interpret code and scope out any possible vulnerability that may exist. Also, scripting is a great tool for automating tasks. This is a testimonial I wrote about my recent experience with She Codes Australia “I'm currently working on my website portfolio project for the HTML/CSS module in the She Codes Australia Plus Program. I have been grateful for the experience the past 3 weeks. This portfolio will only grow more as I develop my coding ability and engage in more projects. I have many aspirations/ passion projects that will greatly benefit from my ability to code well so that's why this opportunity has been one in a lifetime for me. Thank you to all the mentors and lead instructor - Andrew Bloyce. A huge thanks to Kate Kirwin for accepting me into the cohort and giving me a chance to build myself up. I'm looking forward to learning more throughout the 6-month program. I'm now certain that this high-quality practical learning environment has ignited me to aspire to greater heights because of the clear explanations of the content. My peers in the cohort who are a great source of inspiration and support are the cherry on top! I look forward to seeing the deliverables that are created from the cohort.”
